Sagada Tourist Spots

Bomod-ok Falls

This waterfall stands tall and imposing, its cascading waters breathing life into the nearby farms of Pide and Fidelisan.

Church of St. Mary the Virgin

This picturesque church boasts a distinct blend of architectural styles that reflect the local Igorot culture and Western influences.

Hanging Coffins

Hanging Coffins is one of the most popular attractions in Sagada, Philippines. This unique and ancient burial practice involves suspending wooden coffins on the sides of cliffs or inside natural caves.

Marlboro Hills

panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, complete with vibrant green grass, terraced fields, and a backdrop of the Cordillera Mountains.

Ganduyan Museum

Small museum with a private collection of historic indigenous artifacts & educational information.

Obwa Falls

The Blue Lagoon of Sagada or locally known as “Obwa Falls” and about four kilometers south of Sumaguing Cave.

Pongas Falls

Pongas Falls is best visited during the rainy days to catch sight of its full beauty.

Pottery

Secluded house featuring a collection of handmade pottery by local artisans, with hands-on demos.

Sumaguing Cave Sagada

Sumaguing Cave has the biggest chamber, earning its nickname “The Big Cave.” Inside are countless rock formations slowly shaped by nature over thousands of years. These rock formations mimicking familiar shapes are the highlight of this spelunking adventure.
