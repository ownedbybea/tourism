Bontoc Tourist Spots

Mt. Kupapey and Fato

Mt. Fato is known for its countless pine trees, but the most prominent landmark in the hike is the large rock at the summit. Mt. Kupapey -The name of the mountain means “butterfly”, the trail is characterized by a straightforward ascent.

Chico River

Amazing views with lush greens along the river banks. It was good timing it because rainy season was about to end. We had a few stops along the way just to see the views

Humuyo Falls

Humuyyo falls cascade in two layers. Collectively, it measures more than 40 meters high, making it the one of the highest falls in Bontoc.

Mainit Hot Spring

Mainit is known for its mineral springs (sulphur) that provide invigorating baths after a hard day’s work in one’s farm.

Maligcong Rice Terraces

The majestic Maligcong Rice Terraces in Bontoc, Mountain Province is eyeing the distinguished honor of becoming a certified National Cultural Treasure, the same status held by the world-renowned Ifugao Rice Terraces.

Bayyo Rice Terraces

Bayyo Rice Terraces is located along the Bontoc-Banaue Road. You can view this stunning vista along the road. It is around 30-40 minutes of jeepney ride from Bontoc Poblacion.

Bontoc Museum

The museum, designed to resemble an Igorot house, was established by Mother Basil Gekiere and is run by Belgian ICM missionaries to preserve authentic artifacts and photos reflecting the culture of the mountain tribes.

Pusong Lake

Pusong Lake (Forest Lake) , Snaking down thru thick forest was a mountain lake called Pusong.
