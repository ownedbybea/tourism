Welcome to Mountain Province

Discover the breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and unique tourist spots of Mountain Province.

Mountain Province, nestled in the heart of the Cordillera mountain range in northern Luzon, Philippines, is a hidden gem known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unparalleled natural beauty. The province is a perfect escape for nature lovers and adventure seekers, offering a variety of scenic spots that showcase the best of the Philippines' mountainous terrain. With its cool climate, lush forests, and breathtaking vistas, Mountain Province promises a peaceful retreat for those looking to reconnect with nature.

One of the province’s most iconic attractions is the Rice Terraces of Batad, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. These ancient terraces, sculpted by indigenous Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago, are a testament to human ingenuity and sustainable farming practices. The terraces, set against a backdrop of dramatic mountains, offer visitors the chance to experience the unique culture and traditional agricultural practices of the Ifugao. Trekking through Batad gives a deeper appreciation of the historical significance and beauty of these terraced fields.

Another must-see destination is Sagada, renowned for its mystical charm and cool climate. Sagada is famous for its hanging coffins, a centuries-old burial tradition that can be seen in the limestone cliffs surrounding the town. The Sumaguing Cave offers a thrilling underground adventure, where visitors can explore the stalactites and stalagmites within. Sagada also provides opportunities for trekking, including the serene Echo Valley and the Bomod-ok Falls, a majestic waterfall surrounded by lush greenery, offering a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life.

For those seeking panoramic mountain views and cultural immersion, Bontoc, the capital of Mountain Province, offers a mix of historical and natural attractions. The Bontoc Museum provides a fascinating glimpse into the province’s indigenous cultures, showcasing artifacts and historical displays. Visitors can also take in the picturesque views of the Bontoc Rice Terraces, and hike to Mt. Kalaw for a bird’s-eye view of the surrounding valleys. With its combination of rich heritage and breathtaking landscapes, Mountain Province remains one of the Philippines' most captivating and lesser-known travel destinations.
