Quick facts

Mountain Province is a landlocked province of the Philippines in the Cordillera Administrative Region in Luzon. Its capital is Bontoc while Bauko is the largest municipality. Mountain Province was formerly referred to as Mountain in some foreign references. The name is usually shortened by locals to Mt. Province.

Sabangan Mountain Province

Sabangan is a 5th class municipality known for its scenic landscapes, traditional rice terraces, and strong indigenous culture. Its economy is primarily agricultural, with rice, corn, and vegetables being key products. The town is increasingly drawing attention from tourists interested in hiking, cultural experiences, and the beauty of the Cordillera region.

Tadian Mountain Province

Tadian is a 4th class municipality in the province of Mountain Province, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 19,341 people.The town's economy is centered on agriculture, with rice, corn, vegetables, and livestock being key products. Tadian is home to picturesque rice terraces and natural attractions, offering opportunities for hiking and experiencing local traditions. While it is less commercialized than other Cordillera towns, Tadian has potential for eco-tourism, with growing interest in its cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Paracelis Mountain Province

Paracelis, officially the Municipality of Paracelis is a 2nd class municipality in the province of Mountain Province, Philippines. It is celebrated for its agricultural abundance and cultural diversity. Known as the "rice granary" of the province, it produces rice, corn, bananas, and other crops. Paracelis has numerous zigzag roads in most parts, including access roads that connect the municipality to other border towns. On both sides of its zigzag roads, travelers can view the vast cornfield plantations alongside.

Sagada Mountain Province
